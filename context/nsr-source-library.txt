Curated Reading List by Conference Theme

Theme 1: Extending Networks Skyward — Mobile Connectivity &
Convergence

1.1 BCG, “The Next Wave of Telecom Growth: Fixed-Mobile Convergence in Europe,” 2024 - URL:
https://www.bcg.com/publications (search: telecom convergence Europe) - BCG analysis of how ﬁxed-mobile
bundling is reshaping European competitive dynamics, with data on FMC penetration rates across 15 markets.
Directly relevant to convergence narrative at conference. Quantiﬁes the ARPU premium from converged offers
at 15–25%.

1.2 GSMA, “The Mobile Economy Europe 2025,” February 2025 - URL:
https://www.gsma.com/mobileeconomy/europe/ - Comprehensive dataset on 5G rollout, spectrum allocation, and
mobile revenue trends across Western Europe. Includes NTN/satellite-to-device projections. Key reference for
5G SA deployment timelines and the convergence of terrestrial and non-terrestrial networks.

1.3 Ericsson Mobility Report, “Europe Edition,” November 2025 - URL: https://www.ericsson.com/en/reports-
and-papers/mobility-report - Trafﬁc growth forecasts, 5G subscription projections, and FWA adoption data for
Europe. Projects European mobile data trafﬁc to grow 20% CAGR through 2030. Critical for understanding
network capacity arguments driving convergence strategies.

1.4 McKinsey, “The State of 5G: Closing the Gap Between Hype and Reality,” 2024 - URL:
https://www.mckinsey.com/industries/technology-media-and-telecommunications - Assessment of 5G
monetization progress, with sector-by-sector B2B use case analysis. Argues that enterprise 5G revenues remain
below expectations but FWA is a bright spot. Relevant to discussion on extending networks into new verticals.

1.5 Ofcom, “Connected Nations Update,” December 2025 - URL: https://www.ofcom.org.uk/research-and-
data/telecoms-research/connected-nations - UK-speciﬁc data on 5G coverage, shared rural networks, and satellite
connectivity pilots. Key for UK-focused discussions on network extension and convergence.

Theme 2: Capital Strategies 2.0 — Ownership & Partnerships

2.1 Roland Berger, “European Telecom Infrastructure: The Investor Perspective,” 2024 - URL:
https://www.rolandberger.com/en/Insights/ - Analysis of private equity and infrastructure fund activity in
European telecom assets. Maps the ownership shift from integrated operators to carve-out infrastructure vehicles.
Documents €80bn+ in PE-backed telecom infrastructure deals since 2020.

2.2 Oliver Wyman, “The Asset-Light Telco: Myth or Model?,” 2024 - URL: https://www.oliverwyman.com/our-
expertise/industries/communications-media-technology.html - Examines the asset-light vs. asset-right debate,
with case studies from TIM NetCo, BT Openreach, and KPN. Concludes that asset-light works for operators with
scale but creates dependency risks. Directly relevant to TIM’s presence at conference.

2.3 S&P Global Ratings, “European Telecom Outlook 2025–2026,” January 2025 - URL:
https://www.spglobal.com/ratings/en/sector/telecoms - Credit rating trajectory for major European operators,
leverage trends, and capex sustainability analysis. Tracks median net debt/EBITDA across the sector at ~3.2x.
Essential context for capital structure discussions with PE panel.

Theme 3: Monetization Re-Visited — Next Leg of Organic Growth

3.1 BCG, “Winning the B2B Opportunity in European Telecoms,” 2025 - URL:
https://www.bcg.com/publications - Quantiﬁes the €50bn+ B2B digital services opportunity for European telcos
by 2028. Identiﬁes cloud, cybersecurity, and IoT as the three growth vectors. Relevant to TIM Enterprise, BT
Business, and Proximus discussions.

3.2 ETNO, “State of Digital Communications 2025,” March 2025 - URL: https://etno.eu/library/reports.html -
Annual benchmark of European telecom sector performance: revenue, ARPU, capex intensity, and digital service
revenue share. Reports that digital services now account for ~12% of total operator revenue, up from 7% in
2020.

3.3 McKinsey, “Telco Revenue Growth: Five Plays That Work,” 2024 - URL:
https://www.mckinsey.com/industries/technology-media-and-telecommunications - Identiﬁes ﬁve proven
monetization strategies: ARPU upsell, converged bundles, B2B digital, network-as-a-service, and ad-tech/data
monetization. Provides case study evidence from Orange, Deutsche Telekom, and Telenor.

3.4 PwC, “Global Telecom Outlook 2025–2029,” 2025 - URL:
https://www.pwc.com/gx/en/industries/tmt/telecom-outlook-perspectives.html - Projects global telecom revenue
CAGR at ~2.8% through 2029, with European growth lagging at ~2%. Highlights that operators adopting
“TelcOS” AI-native models can achieve 200–400bps margin improvement. Key macro context for monetization
theme.

Theme 4: Networks Meet AI — GenAI & Automation for Telcos

4.1 BCG, “AI-Native Telcos: From Pilots to Platform,” 2025 - URL: https://www.bcg.com/publications -
Framework for telco AI maturity, from experimentation to embedded AI across network ops, customer care, and
ﬁeld service. Estimates AI can reduce opex by 15–25% over 5 years for a typical Western European operator.
Core reference for AI theme.

4.2 GSMA, “AI for Telecoms: From Hype to Impact,” 2025 - URL: https://www.gsma.com/futurenetworks/ai -
Survey of 60+ operators on AI adoption status, with European operators leading in network optimization but
lagging in commercial AI products. Provides useful benchmark data for discussion starters.

strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/ai - Outlines the €20bn EU investment program for AI infrastructure, including
sovereign compute capacity. Relevant to understanding the regulatory push behind AI-ready networks and telco
positioning.

https://www.bain.com/insights/industry/telecommunications - Prioritizes AI use cases by ROI potential: network

Theme 5: Regulation 3.0 — Policy Support & M&A Impact

5.1 EU Commission / DG-Comp, “Competition Policy in the Digital Age — Connectivity Markets,” 2025 -
URL: https://competition-policy.ec.europa.eu/ - Latest competition policy guidance on in-market consolidation,
with updated HHI thresholds and signaling on 4-to-3 mergers. Essential pre-reading for Marc Zedler session.

5.2 The Draghi Report, “The Future of European Competitiveness,” September 2024 - URL:
https://commission.europa.eu/topics/strengthening-european-competitiveness - Former ECB president’s
landmark report calling for telecom consolidation, shift from ex-ante to ex-post regulation, and investment
incentives. The single most referenced policy document at European telecom conferences in 2025–2026.

5.3 Ofcom, “Promoting Investment and Competition in Fibre Networks,” 2024 - URL:
https://www.ofcom.org.uk/consultations-and-statements - UK regulatory approach to balancing ﬁbre investment
incentives with competitive access. Sets the context for UK Fiber Panel discussion on Openreach pricing, altnet
sustainability, and wholesale access.

5.4 Oliver Wyman, “Telecom Regulation in Transition: What Operators Can Expect,” 2025 - URL:
https://www.oliverwyman.com/ - Maps the regulatory evolution from ex-ante price regulation toward ex-post
competition enforcement. Argues this shift will be gradual and uneven across European markets. Good context
for Regulation 3.0 discussions.

Theme 6: Cloud-Native Transformation — Platformization

6.1 BCG, “Cloud-Native Networks: The Telco Transformation Imperative,” 2024 - URL:
https://www.bcg.com/publications - Blueprint for telco cloud-native transformation, including containerized core
networks, CI/CD pipelines, and platform engineering. Estimates that cloud-native operators can reduce time-to-
market for new services by 60%. Core reference for this theme.

6.2 TM Forum, “Cloud-Native Transformation Tracker,” 2025 - URL: https://www.tmforum.org/ - Benchmark
survey of 40+ operators on cloud-native adoption progress. Finds that only ~15% of European operators have
achieved “cloud-native at scale” in core network functions. Useful for grounding discussions in reality.

6.3 McKinsey, “Platform Telcos: Building the Digital Services Stack,” 2024 - URL: https://www.mckinsey.com/
- Analyzes the platform play for telcos: network APIs, developer ecosystems, and digital marketplace models.
Identiﬁes CAMARA network APIs as a potential inﬂection point for telco platform strategies.

Theme 7: Technology Game-Changers — Innovation & Spectrum

7.1 GSMA, “Spectrum for the AI Era: 6 GHz and Beyond,” 2025 - URL: https://www.gsma.com/spectrum/ -
Position paper on 6 GHz allocation for mobile vs. Wi-Fi, with technical analysis and economic modeling. Argues
that mobile allocation of upper 6 GHz is critical for 6G readiness. Central to spectrum discussion.

7.2 EU Commission, “Radio Spectrum Policy: 2025 Review,” 2025 - URL: https://digital-
strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/radio-spectrum - EU-level spectrum policy update covering 6 GHz allocation
timeline, satellite-terrestrial coordination, and spectrum sharing frameworks. Key regulatory reference for
technology theme.
