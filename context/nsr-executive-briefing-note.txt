Executive Brieﬁng Note — NSR/BCG Conference
2026

State of the Industry

European telecom enters 2026 in a cautiously optimistic posture. After a decade of stagnant revenue, eroding
returns, and regulatory constraint, three structural shifts are converging: the regulatory climate is the most
permissive toward consolidation in a generation (Draghi Report, Vodafone-Three precedent), AI is ﬁnally
offering a credible path to 15–25% opex reduction, and ﬁbre/5G investment cycles are approaching their
inﬂection from cash-burn to cash-harvest in several major markets. The sector’s median EBITDA growth of
~2.5% is modest, but the variance between leaders and laggards is widening — operators with disciplined
pricing, enterprise digital capabilities, and advanced network infrastructure are pulling away.

The capital structure of European telecom has been fundamentally rewired. Over €80bn in infrastructure assets
have been carved out, spun off, or sold to PE and infrastructure funds since 2020. TIM’s NetCo sale, Cellnex’s
shift from acquirer to capital returner, and the UK altnet investment boom represent three phases of the same
trend: the unbundling of connectivity into specialized, separately-capitalized layers. Whether this disaggregation
creates lasting value or just redistributes risk is the central debate.

The unresolved tension is growth. European telecom revenue grows at ~2% while the companies it connects
grow at 8–15%. B2B digital services, platform diversiﬁcation (energy, ﬁntech), and network API monetization
are the most-discussed growth vectors — but collectively they still represent <15% of sector revenue. The
conference will test whether the industry’s growth narrative is supported by evidence or remains aspirational.

Three Overarching Narratives That Will Shape the Day

1. “Consolidation works — now do it again.” VodafoneThree is 10 months post-merger and will present
early evidence that 4-to-3 consolidation delivers investment, not just cost synergies. Every speaker and
attendee will be thinking about which European market is next. Marc Zedler’s DG-Comp session will be
parsed for any signal on Brussels’ appetite.

2. “Show me the money on AI.” Every operator has an AI strategy; few have measurable ROI. The

conference will push for speciﬁcity: what has actually been deployed at scale, what are the cash savings,
and what is the roadmap from pilot to platform? BCG’s closing session will attempt to benchmark the
industry.

3. “The capital cycle is turning.” Cellnex returning capital ahead of plan, BT approaching its FCF

inﬂection, TIM restoring dividends — the narrative is shifting from “invest and leverage” to “harvest and
return.” PE investors are evaluating exits, operators are approaching capex peaks, and the market is
rewarding cash generation over growth spending.

My Positioning as an Attendee

As a senior transformation leader at a major Nordic telco, I bring a distinctive perspective: operational
experience in one of Europe’s most digitally advanced markets, familiarity with the consolidation dynamics that
Draghi advocates, and a transformation mandate that bridges technology and commercial strategy. I can add
value to conversations by offering a Nordic benchmark (high penetration, mature pricing, disciplined markets)
against which UK and Southern European operators can compare. I should lean into discussions about AI
operationalization (where Nordic operators often lead), convergence strategy (relevant given Nordic multi-play
dynamics), and regulatory reform (the Nordics as potential next consolidation markets).
