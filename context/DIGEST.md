## 1. Editorial Standards

- **Data-first**: Every claim needs a number, company name, or deal size. Vague assertions are cut.
- **Executive lens**: Frame for infrastructure investors and operators, not journalists or generalists.
- **Regional specificity**: Nordics, UK, Western Europe, SEA, Middle East, and India are distinct markets — never lump them.
- **Forward-looking**: End every article with what this means for the next 6–18 months.
- **Source discipline**: Cite Reuters, FT, Bloomberg, The Information, DC Byte, Structure Research, and company announcements. Analyst reports (McKinsey, BCG, Goldman, PwC, Roland Berger) for benchmarking.
- **Lead with the insight, not the context**: "The Vodafone-Three precedent has opened the floodgates — Goldman now flags Italy, Sweden, and Germany as the next consolidation candidates."

## 2. Article Format (WSJ Style)

Each article follows this structure:

```
## [Declarative headline — most important fact, not a label]
**[One-sentence deck — the so-what]**

[DATELINE CITY] — [Lead paragraph: single most important fact first]

[Nut graf: why this matters for infrastructure investors/operators]

### [Subheading]
[Body: narrative paragraphs, data-dense, named sources]

### [Subheading]
...

**What to watch:** [2–3 sentences on the 6–18 month forward look]

*Sources: [list]*
```

Rules:
- Third person throughout. No dialogue, no hosts.
- Subheadings are declarative statements, not topic labels ("Capacity Doubles in Johor" not "Malaysia").
- Every paragraph has at least one number or named entity.
- Pull the investor implication into the body — don't save it for the end.

## 4. Key Market Reference Data

### European Telecom (ING, Goldman, BCG 2026)
- Sector revenue growth ~2% in 2026; EBITDA growth slightly higher on cost rationalisation
- €80bn+ infrastructure assets carved out to PE/infra funds since 2020
- Consolidation: VodafoneThree approved; Goldman flags Italy, Sweden, Denmark, Germany as next
- AI opex reduction potential: 15–25% (BCG); gap between pilot and production remains wide
- FMC ARPU premium: 15–25% vs standalone; B2B digital services <15% of revenue but fastest-growing

### AI Infrastructure (McKinsey, Deloitte, Futurum 2026)
- Big 4 hyperscaler capex 2026: Amazon $200B, Google $175–185B, Microsoft $110–120B, Meta up to $135B (~$620–640B combined)
- ~75% of aggregate hyperscaler capex funds AI-related infrastructure
- Inference now 55% of AI infrastructure spending (up from 33% in 2023); market >$50B in 2026
- US inland markets (Texas, Midwest) to account for 53% of new hyperscale capacity; grid queue 5–7 years in primary markets

### Fibre & Connectivity (McKinsey 2024)
- ~40% of world population still lacks fibre; 15–25% cost reduction possible through build discipline
- UK altnet sector: CityFibre at 4.7m premises; VMO2 acquiring Netomnia signals consolidation
- Subsea: Meta Project Waterworth — 50,000km, 24 fibre pairs, five continents; work begins 2026

### Energy (McKinsey 2024)
- Energy up to 5% of telco revenue pre-price hikes; UK industrial power ~4× US equivalent rate
- 4 levers for 15–30% savings: RAN sleep modes, AI-driven cooling, PPAs, legacy decommission
- Nuclear PPAs: Meta 6.6 GW (Oklo, Vistra, TerraPower); Microsoft, Equinix active

### Infrastructure Investment (Roland Berger, Macquarie 2024–2026)
- Aligned Data Centers: $40B sale to AIP/BlackRock/GIP/MGX consortium — resets sector ceiling (prior record: AirTrunk $16.6B)
- Blackstone Digital Infrastructure Trust (BXDC): filed April 10, 2026; target ~$2B IPO; 10–20yr hyperscaler leases
- Macquarie model: buy → operate → optimise → exit; AirTrunk 8× entry, Aligned ~60× capacity expansion
- SEA data centre market: $30.47B by 2030; Malaysia power demand 8.5 TWh → 68 TWh by 2030
